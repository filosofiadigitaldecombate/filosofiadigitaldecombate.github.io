Luis Alfonso Castillejo Ventura
A life prepared to serve

I am from Margarita Island, Venezuela. I left at eleven for cancer treatment, and thanks to God, ended up living in Punto Fijo. My father worked at PDVSA in the Amuay and Caron refineries.

I studied Humanities. Philosophy, psychology, sociology, history and music became lifelong passions. In 2000, political debates at home led me to a secret aspiration: to become president of Venezuela one day.

I worked across Venezuela — PDVSA, banks, hotels, restaurants, construction, events. I am a musician, a theologian by study, a professional dancer. In 2015 I trained at the Sea Commandos Unit, completing Commando Course #56.

When I left, my mother Narcisa passed away. Her last words: "Go back to the mother of your daughter." She died smiling, thumb up.

P∆PIR° was born from exile. A Venezuelan in Brazil, phone in hand, faith intact — building the platform where your truth cannot be silenced.
